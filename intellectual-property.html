Trade Marks

A trade mark is a word, phrase, symbol and/or design that identifies and distinguishes the source of the goods of one party from those of others. A service mark identifies and distinguishes the source of a service rather than goods.

Governing legislation

Trade Marks Ordinance 2001Trade Marks Rules 2004

Procedure

1

The application is examined by the Registrar of Trade Marks, which usually takes around 12 months. If objections are raised, the applicant is notified to respond within 2 months.

2

Once the Registrar is satisfied with the written reply, the mark may be approved for advertisement. If not, the applicant is called for a hearing where further amendments may be required.

3

Once cleared of all objections, the mark is advertised in the Trade Marks Journal, inviting any third-party opposition to registration.

4

In the case of no opposition, the mark is then registered.

Requirements

  • ▪Full name, nationality, trading style (if any) and full business address of the applicant.
  • ▪List of goods or services; separate applications for different classes.
  • ▪Transliteration and translation of non-English words appearing in the mark.
  • ▪For a device/label mark, 15 prints of the mark.
  • ▪First date of use of the mark in Pakistan, if used.
  • ▪Power of Attorney signed by the authorised signatory before a Notary Public.
  • ▪For priority, a "convention application" may be filed within six months; priority documents within three months of filing in Pakistan.

Patents

A patent is a grant of exclusive rights for an invention — to make, use and sell it for a limited period of 20 years. Patents are granted for new, non-obvious product or process inventions with industrial application. Patentability criteria in Pakistan include novelty, inventive step and industrial applicability.

Governing legislation

Patents Ordinance 2000Patents Rules 2003

Procedure

1

Receive request to file a patent.

2

Formalities with the client and the Patent Office.

3

Examination at the Patent Office (no fixed time).

4

Acceptance by the Controller of Patents.

5

Published in the IP Journal and/or website.

6

Sealed in the case of no opposition.

7

Renewals.

Requirements

  • ▪Full name, nationality and complete business address of the applicant(s).
  • ▪Two copies of specification and claims in English (A-4 size).
  • ▪Two copies of drawing(s), if any, on A-4 sheets.
  • ▪Application form signed by the applicant(s) and endorsed by the inventor(s) before two witnesses, or an assignment document.
  • ▪Power of Authority signed by the applicant(s) and notarised.
  • ▪For priority, a certified copy of the basic specification filed within three months; priority claimable within 12 months of the first foreign application in WTO member countries.

Types of patent application

Ordinary Application

Created once all criteria to file are met. The official date is the date it is filed at the Patent Office.

Patent of Addition

For improvements or modifications of an invention already applied for or granted.

Convention Application

A priority application dated as of the corresponding application first made in a WTO member country.

Industrial Designs

An industrial design is the ornamental or aesthetic aspect of an article. It may consist of three-dimensional features, such as the shape or surface of an article, or two-dimensional features, such as patterns, lines or colour.

Governing legislation

Registered Designs Ordinance 2000Registered Designs Rules 2023

Procedure

1

Filing of the design application at the IP office.

2

Examination of the application.

3

If all terms are satisfied by the Registrar, it is registered.

4

Issuance of a Registration Certificate, valid for 10 years.

Requirements

  • ▪Full name, nationality and complete business address of the applicant.
  • ▪Representations of the design (line drawings / photographs).
  • ▪Power of Authority signed by the authorised signatory before a Notary Public.
  • ▪For priority, certified documents at filing or within three months (with late fee); priority claimable within six months of the first foreign application in WTO member countries.

Copyright

Copyright is a legal instrument that gives the creator of a work of art, literature or a work that conveys information or ideas the right to control how the work is used — advancing the progress of knowledge by giving authors an economic incentive to create.

Governing legislation

Copyright Ordinance 1962Copyright Rules 1967

Procedure

1

Filing the copyright application.

2

Publication in a leading newspaper within 1 month of filing.

3

Scrutiny or opposition (if any) regarding the application.

4

If opposed, the application status is changed to "pending".

5

Registration in the case of no opposition.

Requirements

  • ▪Full name, nationality and full business address of the applicant.
  • ▪Power of Attorney.
  • ▪Name, business and residential address of the author of the work.
  • ▪The year of creation of the work.
  • ▪Year from which the work was published/used (in Pakistan and worldwide).
  • ▪Affidavit from the author if the applicant is not the author and the work has been assigned.
  • ▪For a logo/artwork, copies on card with author name, applicant name and year of creation (4 sets).

Domain Names

A domain name is a mark which is a user-friendly substitute for an Internet address. The provisions of the Trade Marks Ordinance 2001 apply to domain names, subject to the provisions of the Third Schedule.

Requirements for registration

A domain name used as a source identifier may be registered as a trade mark in respect of relevant goods or services. The applicant must show that they offer goods or services via the internet using the domain name, with a specimen demonstrating use of the domain name as a source identifier.

Note: Use of a domain name as a mere directional reference — similar to a telephone number or business address — is not taken as use as a source identifier.

Key provisions

  • ▪A domain name may indicate the geographical origin of goods or services.
  • ▪It must not be misleading as to its character or significance; the Registrar may require an indication that it is a domain name.
  • ▪The provisions for acceptance, opposition and registration of trade marks also apply to domain names.

Term: registered for 5 years from the date of registration and renewable for further like terms, as long as the domain name is in actual use on the internet.
